# FreightDoc — 08_STARTUP_POTENTIAL.md

## Honest monetization model
- **Pay-per-shipment:** $49–$99 per shipment for occasional exporters.
- **Subscription:** $399/month for high-volume shippers (unlimited
  generations within the plan).
- **White-label:** freight forwarders and customs brokers pay $1,000–
  $3,000/month to offer this to their own clients as a value-add.

## Realistic Year 1 ARR
$80K–$200K, reachable by getting distribution through Amazon FBA seller
communities, DTC brand communities, and freight-forwarder partnerships —
not through paid acquisition.

## Why this monetizes cleanly
Every shipment is a discrete, billable event with a clear, immediate ROI
comparison: $49–$99 vs. a $500–$2,000 forwarder fee or a $10,000 fine for
getting it wrong. The buyer doesn't need to be convinced documentation
matters — they already know; the sale is about convenience and cost.

## Path to scale / exit
Plausible 3–4 year path to $5M ARR through the white-label forwarder
channel. Realistic exit acquirers: Flexport, ShipBob, or a dedicated customs
compliance software company looking to absorb an SMB-friendly front end.

## What NOT to claim
This is not a billion-dollar outcome and should not be pitched as one.
Trade compliance is a real, recurring, fee-generating problem with a large
addressable long tail — a genuine, fundable SaaS business, not a unicorn
narrative. Judges respond better to honest sizing than inflated claims.

## Post-hackathon build priorities if continued
1. Expand beyond 8 corridors to the top 30 global trade lanes.
2. Add a persistence layer (shipment history, saved company profiles).
3. Move to a lower-cost model tier for the classification/generation steps
   once volume grows, keeping a stronger model only for cross-validation.
4. Add e-signature integration for the generated documents.
